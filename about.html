About

Jessica is a recent graduate of Northwestern University with a Bachelor of Arts as a double major in English and German. She completed the fiction-sequence as part of the Creative Writing Program and studied abroad in Summer 2011 and Fall 2012 with support of the DAAD (Deutsche Academische Austausch Diesnt) and Provost Office at Northwestern. She currently works as an editorial intern for Northwestern Univeristy Press and the new literary magazine the Chicagoan while also acting as social media edtior for several graduate departments in the McCormick School of Engineering and Applied Sciences.

In addition to a busy work schedule, Jessica enjoys travel and plans to visit China with a friend and her family following commencement. She also has a habit of stumbling upon odd opportunies, such as house-sitting in a mansion in Manhattan and hosting travelers from as far as Kosovo in her small apartment in Evanston. She collects books and not much else which is not a good choice when traveling and fulfilling miniscule international baggage limits. To learn more about Jessica's professional experience take a look at her resume and feel free to contacther if you feel so compelled. Return to every so often to read new writing from the mind of an ever curious examiner of life.
